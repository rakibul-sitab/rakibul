Project 3 : Customer segmentation for target market strategies using Tableau

September 1, 2022

Case scenerio :

Super store supplies intend to spend on marketing again. This time, they wish to maximize their profit through a targeted approach. In this regard, the marketing team has requested the data expert team to provide some insights on customer behavior in 2017.

RFM :

For the segmentation, RFM analysis have been used in this project. RFM analysis is a data driven customer behavior segmentation technique that is mostly used to segment customers based on their last purchase, how often they purchased and how much they spent.

Dataset :

Superstore data set have been used from Tableau. It contains information about products, sales, profits, and so on with 20 fields and 9994 rows.

Customer have been segmented into 7 groups:

  1. Whipped Cream : Standard customer and purchased very recently.

  2. Champion : Purchased recently, are frequent buyers and higher spenders.

  3. Loyal : These customers orders regularly.

  4. Potential : They purchased recently, spent a significant amount of money, and are repeat buyers.

  5. Promising : This customer segment was a potential loyalist a few months ago. Spends frequently with a good amount.But the last purchase was few months ago.

  6. Needs attention : They purchased few months ago, atleast 2 months ago.

  7. At Risk : This segment is almost lost.They purchased few months ago with smaller frequency and monetary value.

Yearly update (2017):

  1. Total sales was 733K.

  2. Yearly profit ration was 12.7%.

  3. Total 12K quantity have been sold.

  4. Yearly average discount was 15.6%.

Who is our top rated customer ?

  1. They bring about 276K out of 733K and are under the whipped cream group. Seattle, New York City, San Francisco, and Los Angeles are the cities where most of the sales comes from. Additionally, the states of Chicago (profit: €-2.9K) and Philadelphia have experienced some notable losses (Profit : €-1.5K).

  1. In terms of frequency and monetary value, this customer category can be divided into three major clusters. The cluster graph’s upper right quadrant zone, which is depicted in the bottom left graph of the above image, contains the top customers. But out of all customers, Emily Phan (17 times purchased) and Adrian Barton (Total €14K purchases) are the top customers among all customers.

  2. Emily Phan made a total of five different purchases worth €5,478 in the USA, the last of which was made 12 days ago. Adrian Barton made purchases from five different stores. He purchased 41 days ago. His ten purchases brought in a total profit of €341.2 for super store supplies.

Who is our loyal customer ?

Out of $733K, loyal customers contribute a total of €129K. Seattle, San Francisco, Los Angeles, and New York City are the sales hubs. Both Louisville and Burlington suffered some shocking losses. The two most devoted consumers in this group are Ken Black (12 purchases) and Raymond Buch (€15K purchases).

Which customer has the potential to buy more?

They recently purchased, spent a considerable amount of money, and are repeat customers. This group brings in a total of €60,000. The majority of sales are concentrated in Seattle, Los Angeles, and New York City. Some notable losses have been reported in Chicago and Jacksonville. Based on their purchasing habits, this group can be separated into two cluster groups. Becky Martin (bought €12k) is the top potential client in this category.

Which customers is likely to be churned?

They are in the group that need special attention. They bought it a couple months ago, at least two months ago. They are primarily concentrated in Seattle, San Francisco, Los Angeles, New York City, and Tyler. They bought 5 times on average, with an average monetary worth of €1000.

Who have churned already?

We’ve already lost this group. They bought it a few months earlier with a lower frequency and monetary value. They are largely centred in Los Angeles, New York City, and Decatur. They bought four times on average, with an average monetary worth of €1,000.

Marketing Actionable Strategies:

Every customer is unique, and they should be treated differently, and marketing strategies should be different.

  1. Whipped Cream : Limited time offers could be made.

  2. Champion : They can be early adopts of new products. Some rewards could be offered.

  3. Loyal : Higher value products could be upsell to them. They are the true reviewers.

  4. Promising and potential : Some coupon could be offered. They should bring back to the store as soon as possible.

  5. Need Attention : Limited time and personalized recommendation could be offered.

  6. At Risk : Some helpful resourced from website and personalized email could be sent.
