Project 5 : E-commerce product range analysis using Python libraries.

September 16, 2022

Case scenario :

Super store supplies have contacted to data consulting firm. To boost their sales and targeting customers, they are interested to do a product range analysis. They want to see some KPI , such as revenue, average check and so on.

Dataset :

The dataset contains the transaction history of an online store that sells household goods and have been collected from Kaggle.The dataset have 541909 rows and 5268 duplicated rows.

The file ’ecommerce_dataset_us.csv’ contains the following columns:

  1. InvoiceNo — order identifier
  2. StockCode — item identifier
  3. Description — item name
  4. Quantity
  5. InvoiceDate — order date
  6. UnitPrice — price per item
  7. CustomerID

Library used: pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Plotly,scipy.seaborn,scipy,sklearn.NLTK,missingno.

Techniques used: Various data wrangling techniques like dealing with missing values, duplicated rows, data type replacement, data categorization, and outliers detection and removal, A/B testing(Ttest).Using lemmatization technique, whole products have been categorized into 25 categories.

Ex.1 How many people purchase every day, week, and month?

4338 customers made purchases during the specified period. An average of 54 customers per day, 308 per week, and 1064 per month made purchases. 1064 people buy each month, while 4338 buy over the course of the year. Therefore, the same customers made many purchases throughout the year.

Ex 2 : How many orders do customer make during a given period of time?

The business disclosed its sales data for the period of time from 2018-11-29 to 2019-12-07. The company sells a total of 39,2692 products during this time. Additionally, they sold 1287 items daily, 7699 every week, and 32,724 per month on average. We can observe from the dynamic graphs that the company sells the most between weeks 42 and 49. When compared to the rest of the year, sales are at their peak in November and the first few days of December. Maybe this product is being sold in preparation for Christmas.

Ex 3 : How much money do customer bring? (LTV)?

Over the course of their thirteen-month engagement, each client from the first cohort generated, on average, $400 in revenue, assuming a 50% profit rate and taking no marketing expenses into account. The average customer lifetime value is $400 USD.

Ex 4 :What is the average purchase size?

Throughout the year, the average monthly purchase size is 3-3.5 USD. Only in April and May of 2019, it rises to between 3.5 and 4 USD.

Ex 5:When do people start buying (purchase retention)?

In the second month (indexed as 1), sales significantly fall (as shown in the graph), with an average of roughly 62% of clients not making any purchases. The twelveth cohort (2018-11) stands out as an exception and performs better than the other cohorts. One year following the first purchase, a retention rate of 18% is seen. But it’s quite difficult to exactly express that using only the facts. Across the entire matrix, we can see changes in retention over time. This may be due to the nature of the company, where clients make repeat purchases followed by periods of inactivity.

Ex 6: What products sold the most? Which are the most selling category?

The top 10 products and categories sold in this store are shown in the above figures. The most demanded items in this shop are the white hanging heart t-light holder and the three-tiered regency cake stand. Many products were challenging to identify because of improper names in the description because the product categories were manually created using the lemmatization technique. As a result, other categories came in first. However, aside from the first one, the most popular products in this store are those relating to bags and luggage, set-boxes, and heart-shaped categories.

Ex 7: Recommend the most appropriate time to display advertising to maximize the likelihood of customers buying the products?

The key to increasing revenue is marketing. Digital marketing has been increasingly popular in recent years. Future digital marketing initiatives by this business will require clever campaigns based on consumer behavior. I can suggest a few things to them.

  1. Digital marketing should be target-based in order to maximize money and reach the greatest number of clients. According to our study, 12 pm is the busiest hour for purchases at this store. So the most effective period of the day for conversion is from 8 am to 12 pm as of a few hours ago. Therefore, the most frequent commercial advertising should occur during this period to increase sales.

  2. The number of campaigns should be restricted on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday. Therefore, Wednesday and Thursday should have greater advertising.

  3. As stated at the outset of this research, this store sold the most things from early November to mid-December.

Therefore, rather than throughout the entire year, this is when there should be the most campaigns.

Ex 9 : What products are most often sold together?

Pair with key fob , key fob with back door and key fob with shed have been often sold together and most popular.This list could be provided to the machine learning engineer for building algorithm for the recommendation products in the web store.

Ex 10 : Testing Hypothesis : Average Unit price for top selling 20 products and low selling 20 products are the different.

According to the data, there is no difference in the average unit price between the top 20 products and the bottom 20 products. In all instances, a significance level of 0.05 was chosen, concluding that there is a 5% likelihood of a difference even when there isn’t one. Lower levels of significance mean that we need more proof before we reject the null hypothesis.
